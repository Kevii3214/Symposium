Double-Consciousness and Vietnamese-American Culture in the Modern Era
from The Souls of Black Folk
"two souls, two thoughts, two unreconciled strivings; two warring ideals in one dark body, whose dogged strength alone keeps it from being torn asunder." (Du Bois 2)
What is Double-Consciousness
W.E.B. Du Bois depicts double-consciousness as the conflict of two identities within one person. It creates disillusionment with both cultures and causes some to question who they are as a person.
Why does this matter?
As Vietnamese-Americans, we often navigate two distinct cultures: Vietnamese and American. We may speak Vietnamese at home and English in public. We may sometimes feel like an outcast among other Vietnamese relatives or American friends. We experience double-consciousness on the daily.
What can we do?
Embrace both parts of yourself. Navigate the expectations of family and American society while being true to yourself. Do not choose one identity over the other, accept that you are who you are. Do not let anyone sway you otherwise.
Personal Experience
I was often bullied as a child for neither being able to speak English or Vietnamese properly. I was inadequate in both cultures' etiquette. Growing up, I was faced with the harsh reality that I was different from the other kids born here. Instead of choosing one identity over the other, I embraced my identity as a Vietnamese-American. I did not let what others said bother me and embraced my favorite parts of both identities. I may not look, sound, or talk like a Vietnamese or an American, but being a mixture of both is a gift in itself.
Takeaway
BE YOURSELF, EMBRACE BOTH IDENTITIES, MERGE YOUR DOUBLE-CONSCIOUSNESS
